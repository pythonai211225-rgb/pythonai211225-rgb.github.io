Slice & Dice
📋 The Scenario

Meet Slice & Dice

You've been hired to design the database for Slice & Dice — a pizza restaurant that takes orders online and in-store. Customers can build custom pizzas by choosing from the restaurant's pizza menu and adding extra toppings. The restaurant needs to track every order, exactly what was ordered, who ordered it, and the prices that applied at the time — because prices change over time.
🧩 Entities to Identify

What are the tables?

👤 customers Who is ordering?
🧾 orders When & by whom?
🍕 pizzas What can be ordered?
🫑 toppings What extras exist?
🔗 ??? junction Pizzas ↔ Toppings?
🔗 ??? junction Orders ↔ Pizzas?
🔀 Relationship Hints

Cardinality between entities

customers ── 1 : N ── orders
orders ── N : M ── pizzas
pizzas ── N : M ── toppings
📌 Requirements

What the schema must support

  • Each customer can place many orders over time
  • Each order can include multiple pizzas (with different quantities)
  • Each pizza has a base name and a base price
  • Toppings can be added to any pizza — each topping has its own extra cost
  • The schema must track the price at the time of the order (prices change — store the snapshot!)
  • All tables must be in at least 3NF
  • Every many-to-many relationship must be resolved with a junction table
🗺️ How to Approach This
1

Open a design tool — draw.io, dbdiagram.io, or even paper. Don't touch SQL yet.

2

Draw each entity as a box — one box per table. Write column names inside.

3

Draw relationship lines — connect the boxes. Mark 1:N with a crow's foot. Resolve N:M with a junction box.

4

Mark PKs and FKs — underline PKs, dashed-underline FKs. Make sure every FK is on the "many" side.

5

Check normal forms — 1NF: atomic values, 2NF: full key dependency, 3NF: no transitive deps. Then write SQL.

💡 Price Snapshot Hint
If pizza prices or topping prices change over time and you need to know what the customer paid, the price must be stored in the junction table — not looked up from the pizzas table.
Normal Forms Reminder
1NF Atomic values — no comma lists in a cell
2NF Every column depends on the full PK
3NF No non-key column determines another non-key column
